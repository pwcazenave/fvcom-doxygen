48  ! !
49  ! The USG-FVCOM (publicly called FVCOM) was developed by Drs. Changsheng Chen !
50  ! and Hedong Liu at the Marine Ecosystem Dynamics Modeling Laboratory at the !
51  ! School of Marine Science and Technology (SMAST), University of Massachusetts- !
52  ! Dartmouth (UMASSD) and Dr. Robert C. Beardsley at the Department of Physical !
53  ! Oceanography, Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution (WHOI). This code was !
54  ! rewritten in FORTRAN 90/2K, modularized, rendered somewhat understandable, !
55  ! and parallelized by Geoff Cowles at SMAST/UMASSD. FVCOM is being upgraded by !
56  ! the UMASSD-WHOI joint team led by Dr. Chen and Dr. Beardsley. !
57  ! !
58  ! The Development was initially supported by the Georgia Sea Grant College !
59  ! Program for the study of the complex dynamic system in Georgia estuaries. The !
60  ! code improvement has been supported by Dr. Chen's research grants received !
61  ! from NSF and NOAA Coastal Ocean research grants and SMAST fishery research !
62  ! grants. !

64  ! FVCOM is a prognostic, unstructured grid, Finite-Volume free-surface three- !
65  ! dimensional hydrostatic/non-hydrostatic primitive equations, Coastal Ocean !
66  ! circulation Model (Chen et al., 2003; 2006, 2007). The model computes the !
67  ! momentum, continuity, temperature salinity, and density equations and is !
68  ! closed physically and mathematically using user-specified turbulent parameters!
69  ! or turbulent closure submodel. The irregular bottom slope is represented using!
70  ! a generalized terrain-following coordinate transformation with spatially !
71  ! variable vertical distribution (Pietrzak et al., 2002). The horizontal grids !
72  ! comprise unstructured triangular cells. The spatial fluxes of momentum are !
73  ! discretized using a second-order accurate finite-volume method (Kobayashi et !
74  ! al. 1999) in conjunction with a vertical velocity adjustment enforce exact !
75  ! volume conservation in individual control volumes. The flux of scalars (e. g. !
76  ! temperature, salinity) is calculated by the second-order accurate upwind !
77  ! scheme. The finite-volume method (FVM) used in this model combines the !
78  ! advantages of the finite-element method (FEM) for geometric flexibility and !
79  ! the finite-difference method (FDM) for simple discrete computation. Current, !
80  ! temperature, and salinity in the model are computed in the integral form of !
81  ! the equations, which provides a better representation of the conservation laws!
82  ! for mass, momentum, and heat in the coastal region with complex geometry. !
83  ! !
84  ! FVCOM was originally developed for the coastal and estuarine applications. !
85  ! With implementation of spherical coordinates, this model is capable of using !
86  ! for the global ocean. !

140  ! External forces used to drive this model: !
141  ! !
142  ! 1) Tidal amplitudes and phases at open boundaries (initial designs include 8 !
143  ! tidal constituents, more can be added as needed) plus astronomical !
144  ! forcing implemented via gradients of tidal-generating potential for !
145  ! each tidal constituent and various corrections due to the Earth tidal !
146  ! and ocean loading. !
147  ! 2) Wind stress [3 ways: a) uniform wind speed and direction, b) spatially !
148  ! distributed wind velocity field, and c) the weather model output wind !
149  ! fields (NCEP, MM5, WRF, ECMWF). !
150  ! 3) Air pressure gradients [2 ways: weather model output and Hurricane or !
151  ! typhoon model predicted. !
152  ! 4) Surface heat flux [3 ways: a) uniform heat flux, b) spatially distributed !
153  ! heat flux, and c) the weather model-output heat flux fields. All the !
154  ! surface net heat flux and short-wave radiation are needed in the input !
155  ! file. !
156  ! 5) Precipitation via evaporation. !
157  ! 6) River discharges: specify the location and discharge volume, temperature, !
158  ! and salinity. !
159  ! 7) Groundwater input: currently diffused bottom flux only. !
160  ! !
161  ! Note: FVCOM can be run directly by coupling with the weather model (WRF or !
162  ! MM5). This is used in the US Northeast Coastal Ocean Forecast System !
163  ! (NECOFS) (http://fvcom.smast.umassd.edu). Modes use for air-sea coupling!
164  ! is not included in this release. !
165  ! !
166  ! Hydrodynamics initial conditions: !
167  ! !
168  ! The model can be prognostically run for both barotropic and baroclinic cases. !
169  ! !
170  ! Tidal forcing can be added into the system with zero velocity field at initial!
171  ! or specified the 3-D tidal initial velocity field using the model-predicted !
172  ! harmonic tidal currents. !
173  ! !
174  ! Initial fields of temperature and salinity needed to be specified by using !
175  ! either limatological field, real-time observed field or idealized functions. !
176  ! The model has included Gregorian time for the time simulation for tidal !
177  ! currents. !

280  ! What are new for version 3.1? !
281  ! !
282  ! 1) Fully sea-ice coupling. The sea ice model was developed by converting the !
283  ! structured grid CICE into the unstructured grid version called UG-CICE). !
284  ! This is one component of G. Gao's Ph.D. thesis work supervised by C. Chen, !
285  ! R. C. Beardsley and A. Proshutinsky. The code was written by G. Gao with !
286  ! assistance of J. Qi and C. Chen and tested by G. Gao and C. Chen. !
287  ! !
288  ! 2) Non-hydrostatic version. The non-hydrostatic dynamics is added into FVCOM !
289  ! as one component of Z. Lai's Ph.D. thesis work supervised by C. Chen, G. !
290  ! Cowles and R. C. Beardsley. The code was written by Z. Lai with assistance!
291  ! of C. Chen and G. Cowles, and tested by Z. Lai and C. Chen. !
292  ! !
293  ! 3) Fully current-wave interaction. An unstructured grid surface wave model !
294  ! (called FVCOM-SWAVE) was developed by Qi et al. (2008). This wave model !
295  ! is modified from SWAN by converting it to unstructured grid version using !
296  ! the second-order accurate FVCOM algorithms. The implementing FVCOM-SWAVE !
297  ! into FVCOM was initialized by A. Wu at SMAST/UMASSD as one component of his!
298  ! Ph.D. thesis work. The work is supervised by C. Chen at SMAST/UMASSD. The !
299  ! code is re-organized, modified and re-debugged to fit more flexible and !
300  ! general setup of FVCOM by J. Qi and C. Chen. !
301  ! !
302  ! 4) Fully current-wave-sediment interaction. Coupling of wave-sediment was !
303  ! initialized by A. Wu and J. Ge at SMAST/UMASSD as a visiting student !
304  ! project. The code was re-organized, modified and re-debugged by J. Qi and !
305  ! C. Chen when it is implemented into version 3.1. !
306  ! !
307  ! 5) NetCDF Input and output. Version 3.1 uses the NetCDF input and output !
308  ! implemented into FVCOM by David Stuebe at SMAST/UMASSD. The code is also !
309  ! modified to improve the efficiency of inter-node data exchange and model !
310  ! output writing. !
311  ! !
312  ! 6) Semi-implicit solver. In addition to the mode-split version, a semi- !
313  ! implicit solver was implemented into FVCOM by Z. Lai and C. Chen. Using !
314  ! this option requires the installation of PETCs. With this implementation, !
315  ! FVCOM can be run by choosing either mode-split scheme or semi-implicit !
316  ! scheme. The semi-implicit version of FVCOM can significantly increase the !
317  ! computational efficiency for regional and global scale application. !
318  ! !
319  ! 7) Nesting software module. This module is built in FVCOM to allow multi-sub-!
320  ! regional FVCOM domains to be run simultaneously through nesting approach. !
321  ! The module was originally written by P. Xue at SMAST/UMASSD when he and !
322  ! Chen applied FVCOM Kalman Filter to conduct The Observing System Simulation!
323  ! Experiments (OSSEs) in Nantucket Sound. This module was modified by Dave !
324  ! Stuebe at SMAST/UMASSD and implemented into the version 3.0 of FVCOM, and !
325  ! graded by J. Qi and C. Chen at SMAST/UMASSD for current-wave interaction !
326  ! modules in in version 3.1. !
327  ! !
328  ! 8) Dike-Groyne module. This module is implemented into FVCOM to deal with the !
329  ! vertical wall under or above the sea surface. The algorithm was derived by !
330  ! C. Chen at SMAST/UMASSD, and the parallelized code was written and tested !
331  ! by J. Qi, J. Ge and C. Chen at SMAST/UMASSD. !
332  ! !
333  ! 9) SST/SSH assimilation module. A SSH assimilation module is added to include!
334  ! the satellite-altimeter data into FVCOM. The algorithm is the same as that !
335  ! used for SST assimilation. The SST/SSH is merged together by Q. Xu, Z. Lai !
336  ! and C. Chen at SMAST/UMASSD and implemented into version 3.1. C. Chen !
337  ! improved the SST algorithm to avoid the unreal thin layer caused by SST !
338  ! nudging. !
339  ! !
340  ! 10) Kalman Filter Assimilation modules. The Kalman Filter package is upgraded !
341  ! by adding a Signular Evolutive Interpolated Kalman filter (SEIK). The !
342  ! source code of SEIK code is provided by Dr. Lars Nerger at AWI, Germany. !
343  ! In addition to Reduced Rank Kalman Filter (RRKF), Ensemble Kalman Filter !
344  ! (EnKF), we also reconstruct Ensemble Transform Kalman Filter (EnTKF) for !
345  ! the OSSEs application. The Kalman Filter assimilation modules were !
346  ! originally developed by a team effort led by C. Chen at SMAST/UMASSD and P.!
347  ! Rizzoli/MIT. The team members include P. Xue, Z. Lai, Q. Xu and J. Qi at !
348  ! SMAST/UMASSD, J. Wei and S. Lyu at MIT and R. C. Beardsley at WHOI. The !
349  ! package is upgraded and implemented into version 3.1 by P. Xue, J.Qi and !
350  ! C.Chen. Dr. Lars Nerger owns the copyright of SEIK method used in FVCOM. ! !
351  ! !
352  ! 11) Optimal Interpolation Assimilation Module. This module is implemented into!
353  ! FVCOM to integrate vertical profiles of hydrographic/current data into the !
354  ! assimilation. The code was written by Q. Xu, J. Qi and C. Chen at !
355  ! SMAST/UMASSD. !
356  ! !
357  ! 12) The pre-process programs to convert the input from the old version of !
358  ! FVCOM to version 3.0 or up. !
359  ! !
360  ! 13) Bugs in the wet/dry treatments on heat flux at the wet/dry boundary are !
361  ! corrected. The bug was detected by T. Kim and fixed by C. Chen and J. Qi at!
362  ! SMAST/UMASSD. The second-order limiter for advection terms in the wet/dry !
363  ! treatment was originally coded in Fortran 77 version of FVCOM. This limiter!
364  ! is added back to updated version 2.6 or up of FVCOM by C. Chen and J. Qi at!
365  ! SMAST/UMASSD. !
366  ! !
367  ! Bugs in selecting General Turbulence Module is corrected by J. Qi and C. !
368  ! Chen at SMAST/UMASSD. !
369  ! !
370  ! Non-general definitions and commands in arrays, variables and NetCDF !
371  ! modules are changed by G. Cowles and Z. Lai to allow to use the version 3.1 of!
372  ! FVCOM on IBM super computer. These problems only occur in version 3.0 or up. !
373  ! The bugs in PETCs were detected by Z. Lai, C. Chen and J. Qi at SMAST/UMASSD !
374  ! and corrected by the PETCs development team. !
375  ! !
376  ! Bugs in the Lagrangian-tracking in the generalized terrain-following !
377  ! coordinates are corrected. Many minor bugs are also corrected. !
